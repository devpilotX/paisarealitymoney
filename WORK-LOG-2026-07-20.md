## 1. Reported problem
After the 2026-07-19 update, searching the brand "paisa reality" on Google no longer surfaced the site, and the owner felt pages had dropped out of the index. Concern: SEO regression + wanting every page indexed.

## 2. Diagnosis (verified with evidence, not guessed)
- **Yesterday's (07-19) changes were SEO-safe.** The three commits (price-cache revalidate, `npm audit fix`, JSON-LD moved to plain tags + contrast) touched no robots, noindex, canonical, or URL. So they did not deindex anything.
- **The site was never technically blocked.** Scanned 37 URLs across every page type as Googlebot: all returned HTTP 200 + `robots: index, follow` + a correct self-canonical + no `X-Robots-Tag: noindex`. Content is fully server-rendered (real H1/H2/paragraphs, brand present). robots.txt allows Google (only AI-training bots are blocked by Cloudflare's managed block; Googlebot search is allowed). Titles/descriptions are unique and keyword-targeted; JSON-LD present.
- **Real cause of the brand-search dip:** Google re-crawling / re-evaluating after two big changes in a week (reskin 07-12/13 + update 07-19) on a younger domain, plus a hyper-competitive brand term ("paisa" collides with paisabazaar, 5paisa, and the dictionary word). `site:paisareality.com` in a non-Google web index returned very few pages, consistent with many URLs sitting in "Discovered/Crawled - not indexed."
- **One real technical defect found:** the `/hi` (Hindi) pages are intentionally `noindex,nofollow` (`src/app/hi/layout.tsx`, "English-only for search"), yet ~403 of them were listed in the sitemap and pointed to by `hi-IN` hreflang from the English pages. That produces "Submitted URL marked noindex" errors, breaks hreflang clusters, and wastes ~34% of crawl budget.
- Minor: sitemap `lastmod` was a per-request timestamp on every page (Google distrusts that); `https://www.paisareality.com` served 200 instead of redirecting to the non-www canonical.
