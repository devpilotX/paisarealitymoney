## Guiding principle (non-negotiable for a YMYL money site)
100% white-hat. Quality and relevance over volume. A few links from real finance
publications beat hundreds of directory links, and manipulative links (paid,
PBN, spam) can actively hurt a young domain. Every tactic here is earn-based or
built on genuine relationships.

## 2. Directories and listings (minor, foundational; do once, then move on)
Skip mass "free directory submission" services (spam signal). Worth doing:
- Product Hunt: launch the Real Return Checker and Money Health Score as tools.
- AlternativeTo and moderated tool directories: list the calculators.
- Curated, editorially-moderated fintech / "Made in India" tool lists only.
- Google Business Profile: only if a real business entity exists (brand/NAP, not SEO).
- Wikipedia/Wikidata: long game; scheme pages may become citations if genuinely authoritative. No self-promotion.

## 3. Digital PR - the biggest opportunity (we sit on data)
- Mis-selling expose (flagship): run the Real Return Checker across common
  endowment/money-back policies; publish the aggregate finding. Pitch to ET Wealth,
  Mint/LiveMint, Moneycontrol, BusinessLine, Value Research, Freefincal, Cafemutual.
- Seasonal gold data (recurring): around Dhanteras, Akshaya Tritiya, and wedding
  season, publish "gold price trend across N cities" from our data; pitch national
  and regional/language press (regional desks love city-specific numbers).
- Fuel/LPG city comparisons: consumer/regional desks.
- Budget and scheme news: publish fast, comprehensive explainers when a scheme
  launches or the budget drops; our schemes DB makes us fast to be a source.
- Journalist-query platforms (post-HARO; HARO/Connectively shut down Dec 2024):
  Qwoted (finance writers favour it), Featured.com, Source of Sources (free).
  Answer relevant queries with expert commentary. Highest-reliability ongoing tactic.

## 4. Guest posts and partnerships (selective, not at scale)
- A few real relationships, not mass cold email. Contribute practical, tool-tied
  how-tos to mid-tier PF blogs and education/career portals (for scholarships).
- Angles that get accepted: "How to spot a mis-sold insurance policy (with a free
  checker)," "How to check which govt schemes you qualify for," "Small-savings
  rates this quarter, explained."
- Partnerships: financial-literacy NGOs, college/education platforms (scholarships/schemes).
- Communities (genuine value only): r/IndiaInvestments, r/personalfinanceindia,
  Quora. Link a tool only when it directly answers the question. Never drop-and-run.
- Unlinked-mention reclamation: Google Alert for "Paisa Reality"; request a link
  when mentioned without one.

## 5. What to avoid (protect the YMYL profile)
Buying links, PBNs, link farms, mass/low-quality directories, paid do-follow
guest-post networks, comment/forum-signature spam, reciprocal-link schemes,
exact-match anchor over-optimization. A clean profile beats a big one.

## 6. 90-day sequence
- Weeks 1-2 (foundation): Product Hunt launches (both tools); a few quality tool
  listings; set up Google Alerts; sign up for Qwoted / Featured / Source of Sources.
- Weeks 3-6 (linkable assets): produce the mis-selling study + a seasonal gold-data
  piece; answer journalist queries weekly; build a 20-30 target list.
- Weeks 6-12 (outreach): pitch the studies; pursue resource-page inclusions; 2-4
  quality guest posts; convert unlinked mentions; engage communities.

## 7. KPIs
Referring domains (GSC Links report + a free Ahrefs/Ubersuggest tier), and
branded search volume for "paisa reality" (a rising brand-search trend signals the
outreach is landing). Track monthly. Expect a slow compounder: meaningful
referring-domain growth over 2-3 months, not days.

# Appendix A: Mis-selling data study (flagship linkable asset) - outline

Goal: a defensible, public-interest data story that journalists cite.

Method (honest, reproducible):
1. Pick a representative set of real, commonly-sold products: traditional
   endowment plans, money-back plans, and any "double your money in N years"
   pitches. Use publicly stated premium/benefit illustrations.
2. For each, compute the real annualised return (XIRR) using the Real Return
   Checker. Record assumptions (premium, term, maturity/benefit, bonuses).
3. Aggregate: median and range of real returns; compare against PPF, an index
   fund, and inflation. Show how many fall below inflation.
4. Publish a transparent methodology section (link to /methodology) and let
   readers reproduce each result in the tool. Cite sources for every figure.

Deliverable page: a study/report page (e.g., `/guides/insurance-real-returns`)
with a clear chart, the tool embedded/linked, and downloadable/quotable stats.

Headline options (fill with the real computed number):
- "We ran [N] popular insurance plans through an XIRR check. The median real
  return was [X]% - below PPF and barely above inflation."
- "[Y] out of [N] endowment plans we tested returned less than inflation."

Do NOT invent numbers. Compute them, show the working, and let anyone verify.
