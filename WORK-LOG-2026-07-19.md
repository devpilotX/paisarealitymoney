## 1. The reported problem
Live prices were showing "16 July" when the date was 19 July. The site was not appearing to update daily.

## 2. Root cause (verified, not guessed)
The cron and database were healthy the whole time. The database had fresh daily rows for gold, silver, fuel, and LPG (all `MAX(price_date) = 2026-07-19`, continuous back to 12 July). Two schedulers run the update successfully:
- `mcpagent` crontab: curl to the cron endpoint at 06:20, 12:20, 18:20 IST.
- systemd `paisareality-prices.timer` at 07:00 IST.

The stale date was a **frontend caching issue**. The price pages use Next.js ISR (`export const revalidate`, 900s on gold/silver/petrol/diesel, 3600s on lpg, 300s on home/newsletter) with `stale-while-revalidate` of about one year. On a low-traffic site the first visitor is served the last prerendered snapshot while a fresh copy regenerates in the background, so the visible "as of" date lagged by days. Cloudflare was not caching the HTML (`cf-cache-status: DYNAMIC`), confirming it was the Next.js full-route cache.

Prices come from:
- Gold and silver: live spot from gold-api.com plus USD/INR from frankfurter.dev, converted with 6% duty + 3% GST.
- Petrol and diesel: live daily from CarDekho, with a verified baseline fallback.
- LPG: baseline plus admin overrides (revises monthly).
